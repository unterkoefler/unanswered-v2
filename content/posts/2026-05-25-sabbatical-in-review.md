After five grueling years of labor in the software mines
at Notarize, Inc., d.b.a. Proof.com, I was granted a brief respite
in the form of a month-long sabbatical. I took off from Wednesday March 4th
to Sunday April 5th (inclusive). (Also, I have since left the company for good,
but more on that next time.)

My ambitions for this month were moderate:
1. [Run a 34-mile trail race](/slipsliding)
2. Write a novel
3. Travel the world
4. Experience everything that New York City has to offer
5. Veg out for a couple days

Here's how I did:
1. TOTAL FAILURE! I did not run the race. I had to stop training in February due to heel
pain and knee pain. I am now doing P.T.. My doctor thinks my tight, lying hips are to
blame. Maybe they will be loose and honest enough to run the race next year...
2. TOTAL FAILURE! I wrote nothing! Not one word. Too much free time, I think, but also
not enough.
3. NEAR-TOTAL FAILURE! I traveled to Utah and then Florida. Not quite the entire world, but
who needs that when you have good friends, rocks, stars, and alligators.
4. NEAR-TOTAL FAILURE! Alas, I did not make it to every museum, event or going-on in the
city. But I went to the Frick, Central Park, the Brooklyn Museum and the Brooklyn
Botanic Garden. Pretty neat.
5. SUCCESS! I exhibited extremely vegetable-like behaviors for several days before and
after my travels. One day I spent hours upon hours on my computer playing
[this dumb game](https://thomaswc.com/2025.html).
